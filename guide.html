EAHA Operations Manual

Welcome to the Elys Astra Helper Application. This system is a self-contained, highly advanced tactical suite designed to provide immediate analytical advantages, offline record-keeping, and rapid LFG deployments for Path of Titans survival operations.

1. Core Architecture & Data Preservation

Unlike standard web applications, EAHA does not connect to a remote cloud server. Your privacy and data integrity are absolute. The application operates using a high-capacity IndexedDB engine localized entirely within your current web browser.

System Directive: Routine Backups Because your browser acts as the server, clearing your browser's history and cache will permanently incinerate your EAHA database. You must navigate to the JSON Backup tab and utilize the "Export Personal Backup" protocol regularly to secure a hard copy of your files.
  • Merge Official Updates: When server administrators provide an updated JSON.json file, clicking "Check for App Updates" will safely inject the newest server rules and dinosaur profiles into your database without overwriting your personal combat logs or custom map markers.
  • Factory Reset: This protocol will purge your localized database and forcibly rebuild it from the ground up using only the base JSON.json file. Use this if the system experiences a fatal crash.

2. The Lifeline Dashboard

The Dashboard is your active survival HUD. It tracks your current session's vitals, milestones, and immediate tactical needs.

  • Active Dinosaur Selection: Engaging the dropdown will pull your designated creature from the database. This automatically populates the dashboard with their specific Dietary needs, Habitats, Upkeep tasks, and their Adult Combat Arsenal (Base Damage and True DPS).
  • Vital Decay Timers: Enter a minute value (e.g., 60) and initialize the countdown. Your Comfort, Hygiene, and Satiation bars will simulate decay. Clicking Reset Timer pauses the countdown without erasing your current percentages, allowing you to manually adjust them after a successful mud roll or hunt.
  • Eldering Protocol: Log your successful migrations. The UI automatically tracks your progress toward the 16-migration Rebirth token threshold.
  • Quick Log: Instantly record a Victory, Defeat, or Starvation event. The engine automatically tags the event with your currently active dinosaur and files it into the Combat Analytics database.

3. Profiles & The Raw Matrix Importer

The Creature Profiles tab is where you construct the database that feeds the rest of the application's math engines.

✨ Magic Auto-Fill (OCR)

Paste or drop a screenshot of a creature's Discord profile card directly into the designated zone. The Optical Character Recognition (OCR) engine will filter out visual noise, automatically crop the avatar to a perfect square, and extract the Name, Tier, Diet, Habitats, and Behaviors to save you from manual data entry.

⚡ 5-Stage Data Matrix Importer

To power the Threat Assessor, you must import the game's actual code. Paste raw configuration variables into the Matrix Importer. The engine uses a Regular Expression scanner to hunt for arrays formatted exactly like this:

BiteDamage",Values=(20,30,40,50,60))
Core.MaxHealth",Values=(150,262.5,375,487.5,600))
  • Order Independence: You can paste massive, unorganized walls of text. The engine will accurately pluck out the data it needs.
  • The 5-Stage Array: Values represent the Path of Titans growth stages: Hatchling, Baby, Adolescent, Sub-Adult, Adult.
  • Smart Sorting: Core attributes (Health, Weight, Speed, Stamina, Armor, Capacity) are mapped to the primary grid. All other recognized values are generated as Custom Mechanics. These are further categorized—stats containing terms like "Damage" or "Bleed" are promoted to the red Primary Mechanics dropdown, while obscure data is filed under Advanced Variables.

4. Threat Assessor Engine & Sandbox

The Stats tab houses the application's most advanced mathematical algorithms, serving as a dynamic battle simulator that mirrors the backend code of Path of Titans.

The Sandbox Calculator

Select a stat (e.g., Health) and input a percentage modifier (e.g., +15% for a Lone Survivor buff). The engine unpacks the creature's entire 5-stage growth array, calculates the buff simultaneously across all life stages, and outputs the brand-new matrix.

Behind the Scenes: How the Threat Assessor Works

When you select an Attacker, a Defender, and their respective Growth Stages (Hatchling to Adult), the engine isolates the precise integer for that life stage from the matrix and executes the following game-accurate calculations:

  1. Combat Weight & Armor Deflection:
    The absolute core of PoT math. The engine takes the Attacker's Weight, divides it by the Defender's Weight, and then factors in the Defender's Base Armor modifier. Formula: (Attacker CW / Defender CW) / Defender Armor. This generates the master Damage Multiplier (e.g. 150%).
  2. The Arsenal Breakdown (True DPS Math):
    The simulator doesn't just look at one bite. It scans the Attacker's entire matrix for any variable containing "Damage" (e.g., TailDamage, KickDamage, BiteDamage). It then hunts for its matching cooldown (e.g., TailCooldown). It runs the Base Damage through the Combat Weight Multiplier to find the Estimated Hit HP, and then divides that by the Cooldown to give you the True DPS (Damage Per Second). The results are sorted with the most lethal attacks at the top.
  3. Sprint Endurance Engine:
    Stamina pools don't tell the whole story. The engine locates Core.MaxStamina and divides it by StaminaSprintCostPerSecond. This outputs the exact, real-world seconds a dinosaur can sprint before gassing out, allowing you to gauge if you can out-endure a pursuit.
  4. Agility & Turn Radius Scanners:
    The engine checks TurnRadiusMultiplier for both combatants. If the Defender has a superior turn multiplier, it generates an Agility Warning to caution you against getting tail-ridden.
  5. Status Effect Badges:
    The simulator looks for custom mechanics like BleedAmount, BoneBreakChance, Venom, or Poison. If these values are greater than 0 at the selected growth stage, it generates a visual threat badge on the dinosaur's card.
  6. The Tactical Banner:
    Finally, the engine weighs your total DPS against your Combat Weight advantage, combining them into a final AI-generated tactical verdict (e.g., "OVERWHELMING ADVANTAGE" or "LETHAL THREAT").

8. Troubleshooting & Diagnostics

Q: My lists are empty and the system isn't saving my profiles!
A: Ensure you are not operating in "Incognito" or "Private Browsing" mode, which strictly prohibits the IndexedDB storage engine from functioning. If the issue persists on a normal browser, execute a Factory Reset in the JSON Backup tab.

Q: The Matrix Importer ignored a stat I pasted.
A: The parser strictly demands the exact server syntax: Name",Values=(1,2,3,4,5)). If the line is missing a parenthesis, comma, or the quote mark after the name, the Regex scanner will intentionally bypass it to prevent database corruption.

Q: I'm in the Map Room, but I can't click or drag my markers anymore.
A: You are likely locked in "Draw Route" mode. Click the "🤚 Navigation" button in the top toolbar to re-engage standard interaction protocols.

Q: The OCR Auto-Fill is extracting gibberish or merging words incorrectly.
A: The OCR engine reads raw pixel data. If your Discord screenshot contains complex background UI elements or reaction emojis, the engine will attempt to translate them into text. Crop your screenshot tightly around the text block before importing.

Q: The Threat Assessor says "No primary damage stats found in matrix."
A: The simulator hunts specifically for custom variables containing the word Damage. If your dinosaur's imported code is missing those lines, or they are spelled differently in the server files without "Damage" in the title, the engine cannot auto-calculate the DPS.
